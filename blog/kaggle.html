What happens when you mix competition and data science? Kaggle!
Kaggle is a site that offers a public platform to machine learning problems to any levels, from begginers to experts.
Kaggle also offers many different services:

  • Kaggle Kernels: a cloud-based workbench for data science and machine learning. Allows data scientists to share code and analysis in Python and R. Over 150K "kernels" (code snippets) have been shared on Kaggle covering everything from sentiment analysis to object detection.
  • Public datasets platform: have an idea but don't have a good dataset? Kaggle community members got you covered, community members share datasets with each other. Has datasets on everything from bone x-rays to results from boxing bouts - currently 47,679 datasets.
  • Kaggle Learn: for short-form AI education, from january 2020 everyone who finishes a course will now receive a completion certificate.
  • Jobs board: employers post machine learning and AI jobs.

How do I even start?

Kaggle can be looking very intimidating - some competition can be against teams of experienced Ph.D researchers, many subjects, dataset, courses etc. How do I even start?
